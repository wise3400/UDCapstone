Why Apply Universal Design to Websites?

Applying Universal Design to websites is to ensure equal access, improvements in user experience, and meeting legal requirements. By making websites accessible to all users, including those with different types of disabilities, Universal Design undoubtedly promotes inclusiveness and ensures that everyone can access information and services online.


When we encounter this approach, it is certain that it will enhance the overall user experience. Not by one individual who has a disability, but to all users. This is all done by making the website more intuitive, user-friendly, and mobile first meaning it will work on different types of devices (e.g. tablets, phones, laptops). When we have designed an accessible website, it often performs better in search engines. This will benefit all types of businesses by expanding and reaching out to customers. For sure, this will improve customer satisfaction. To sum up, the main purpose of Universal Design is to accommodate diverse user needs and just simply give all people equal access.

Accessibility for Everyone

Universal Design stresses to make environments, products, and services usuable by individuals with diverse abilities. This includes disabilities, elderlies, and individuals with permanent and temporary impairments. By applying human-centered design which is considering a wide range of user needs and preferences, Universal Design enhances accessibility and promotes independence.

Accessibility Standards and Guidelines

Web Content Accessibility Guidelines or known as WCAG developed by the World Wide Web Consoritum (W3C), provide sets of recommendations for creating accessible web content. Guidelines will cover aspects such as providing alternative text for images, smooth keyboard navigation, and maintaining good color contrast. They align closely with Universal Design principles and focuses attention on the creation of universally accessible digital experiences.

Far Better User Experience

Yes. Universal Design enhances user experiences for everyone by a lot. Web developers must priortize simplicity, clarity, and flexibility in design. Universal Design improves simply improves usability and satisfaction for all users. This will lead to better outcomes and increases engagement. People won't be bored!

Don't Break the Law

You must abide by the law. Universal Design is lead by legal requirements and ethical principles aimed to promote accessibility and nondiscrimination. The United States Congress has passed the American with Disabilities Act or ADA which is a legislation that mandates accessibility standards in public accomodations. The United Nations has also developed a "Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities" which is an international human rights treaty that adocates for the rights of individuals with disabilities.

Summary

To have on-going improvement, developers must embrace user feedback through iteration of human-centered design and developing technologies. The concept is to both encourage designers and developers to remain on high alert in addressing accessibility issues and seek creative solutions to enhance inclusiveness.

The key to success is by applying principles of universal design and standards / guidelines such as WCAG and the law. By doing this, organizations, businesses, services, etc. can create an environment that is user-friendly, accessible, useful, and most importantly to welcome to all people.
